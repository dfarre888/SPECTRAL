# Bayraktar TB2

Group : Reconnaissance & Strike Drones

Status : Active

Also Known As : TB-2

Origin : ![](https://www.deagel.com/img/flags/c0203.png)**Turkiye**

Contractor : Baykar

Initial Operational Capability (IOC) : 2016

First Flight : August 2014

Total Production : ?

Unitary Cost : USD $5.0 million

![](https://www.deagel.com/library/sm/2020/m02020100600004.jpg)

The TB2 Bayraktar is a tactical reconnaissance and strike unmanned air vehicle (UAV) developed by Baykar to meet the requirements of the Turkish Armed Forces. Its concept is very similar to the United Sates Predator/Reaper aircraft family with the ability to conduct Intelligence, Surveillance and Reconnaissance (ISR) as well as and strike missions. The unmanned air vehicle first flew in August 2014 and became operational by 2016. It has been deployed by Turkey in Syria and in other conflicts with a few countries buying the aircraft in Africa, Eastern Europe and the Middle East. It has been widely used in Libya and during the war between Armenia and Azerbaijan in the Caucasus in 2020.

The TB2 Medium Altitude Long Endurance (MALE) drone can be armed with up to four laser-guided MAM-C and MAM-L missiles developed and manufactured by Turkey’s Roketsan. The UAV can be equipped with an array of electro-optical (EO) and Infrared (IR) sensors as well as laser designator. Baykar claims that the TB2 can load and operate multi-mode Active Electronically Scanned Array (AESA) radar.

# Bayraktar TB3

Group : Reconnaissance & Strike Drones

Status : Under Development

Origin : ![](https://www.deagel.com/img/flags/c0203.png)**Turkiye**

Contractor : Baykar

Initial Operational Capability (IOC) : 2026

First Flight : October 27, 2023

Total Production : ?

![](https://www.deagel.com/library/sm/2026/m02026021900014.jpg)

The Bayraktar TB3 is a further version of the TB2 optimized for taking off and landing from short and unprepared runways as well as from vessels such as amphibious assault ships. The TB3 features a foldable wing structure and can perform both attack and reconnaissance missions from long distances using both line-of-sight (LOS) and beyond line-of-sight communications. The TB3 performed its first flight on October 27, 2023 and the first flight from an aircraft carried on November 19, 2024. The TB3 may enter service with the Turkish Navy in 2026.
